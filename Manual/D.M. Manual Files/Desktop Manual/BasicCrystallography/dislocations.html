Dislocations and Image Contrast: Dislcoations are a type of line defect in a crystal. They have displacement fields which are
continuously distributed througout the crystal. Since contrast in an electron micrograph will
depend on the relative displacment of atoms about their position with respect to a perfect lattice,
the complex nature and long range perturbations generated by a dislocation leads to complex images in an electron micrograph. Dislocations can be characterized by the Burgers vector b and the dislocation line direction u.
